Pre-Primary Class Curriculum

The Child Centric Curriculum in Pre-Primary education, based on the Play-way method, focuses on activity-based learning tailored to each child’s potential and interests. In addition to the diverse activities and hands-on learning experiences, the curriculum emphasizes the importance of community and social interaction. Daily morning assemblies, interactive classroom technology, and various celebrations and events foster a sense of belonging and engagement among students. This holistic approach ensures that each child not only develops academically but also grows socially and emotionally, preparing them for future educational endeavors.


















Following are the Activities Included in the Curriculum:
1) Diverse Activities: Includes painting, singing, dancing, moral & values education, sports, storytelling, karate, and various competitions.
2) Hands-On Learning: Promotes first-hand experiences through a variety of hands-on activities, accommodating different learning styles.
3) Morning Assembly: Daily assemblies to start the day with a sense of community and routine.
4) Outdoor Play: Swings, climbers, slides, and nature walks to encourage physical activity and exploration.


















5) Activity Room: Equipped with games like ball pool, puzzles, role play, flash cards, teaching tools, clay zone, projector, and storybooks.
6)Technology Integration: Computer literacy programs and interactive boards with speakers in all classrooms.
7) Celebrations and Events: Festivals, special programs, birthday celebrations, grandparents’ day, winter carnival, sports day, annual day, and annual concert ensuring 100% participation.
8) Exhibitions and Outings: Annual art and craft exhibitions, excursions, outings, and picnics to broaden learning experiences.
